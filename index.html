About Me

I am a second year PhD student in the Division of Computational & Data Sciences at Washington University in St. Louis. I am interested in applying machine learning and data-driven approaches to study neurological and neuropsychiatric disorders. With my advisor, Aristeidis Sotiras, I aim to apply machine learning methods (particularly Non-Negative Matrix Factorization) to multimodal neuroimaging data in Alzheimer's Disease.

I have a background in wet lab & clinical neuroscience research. I studied biology and neuroscience at Grinnell College, as well as psychiatric research at King's College London. I have also held research assistant positions at Washington University and the National Institutes of Health.

Outside of lab, I enjoy making & listening to music, working on Python projects, playing games of all kinds, and spending time with my partner and our dogs.
